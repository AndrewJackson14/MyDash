2. Scope

This policy applies to:

  • The MyDash staff application at mydash.media.
  • The client-facing portal and upload pages used by advertisers, legal-notice filers, and other partners.
  • Newsletter sign-ups, reader accounts, and contact forms on the consumer websites we publish (operated via our StellarPress platform).

It does not apply to third-party websites we link to, or to the editorial content of our publications themselves (which are governed by their own publication policies).

3. What we collect

Directly from you

  • Account information — name, role, work email address, phone number, and profile photo.
  • Client / advertiser information — business name, contact names and titles, billing address, payment method (tokenized by our payment processor — we do not store full card numbers), purchase history, and communications with our sales staff.
  • Content you upload — ad creatives, proofs, logos, press releases, legal-notice documents, and any other files you submit through the client portal or provide to our staff.
  • Email and messaging content — messages exchanged through MyDash's internal discussion threads, and emails you send us that are imported for record-keeping.
  • Newsletter subscriptions — email address, optional name, and preferences you select.

Automatically, when you use MyDash

  • Session and device data — browser type, IP address, authentication tokens, session timestamps, and basic error/diagnostic information to keep the platform running.
  • Usage data — pages viewed, actions taken (e.g. story drafts, ad project updates), and time stamps. Used for audit trails, troubleshooting, and product improvement.

From third parties

We import business-relevant records from integrated services you authorize (see Section 6): payment and invoice records from QuickBooks, email threads and calendar events from Google Workspace when connected, and similar. We do not purchase personal data from data brokers.

4. How we use it

We use personal data to:

  • Operate the MyDash platform and provide the services our staff and clients rely on it for.
  • Manage accounts, authentication, permissions, and audit trails.
  • Process payments, generate invoices, and reconcile accounting records.
  • Send transactional messages (proof approvals, invoice delivery, renewal notices, receipts) and, where you've opted in, marketing or newsletter messages.
  • Investigate and resolve security, fraud, abuse, and operational issues.
  • Comply with legal obligations, respond to lawful requests, and defend legal claims.
  • Improve the platform — bug triage, feature prioritization, and occasional aggregate analytics.

We do not sell personal data. We do not share personal data with advertisers or data brokers for their own marketing purposes.

5. Legal basis

For U.S. residents, we process personal data based on the contract you or your employer has with us, our legitimate business interests (e.g. keeping the platform secure, maintaining an audit trail), your consent where we ask for it (e.g. newsletter opt-in), and our legal obligations.

7. Retention

  • Active records — retained while the account or business relationship is active, and for as long as needed for accounting, legal, and audit purposes (generally up to 7 years for financial records in line with U.S. tax requirements).
  • Content uploads — retained while the related ad project, story, or legal notice remains on our systems, and for a reasonable archival period after.
  • Newsletter subscriber data — retained until you unsubscribe, plus a short suppression period to honor the unsubscribe.
  • Security, audit, and system logs — retained for the period needed to investigate incidents, typically 12–24 months.
  • Backups — encrypted backups are retained on a rolling window. Deleting a record from production results in its removal from backups within the backup rotation period.

8. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to:

  • Know what personal data we hold about you and request a copy.
  • Correct inaccurate personal data.
  • Delete personal data (subject to legal / accounting retention exceptions above).
  • Opt out of marketing and newsletter communications at any time (every newsletter has an unsubscribe link).
  • Designate an authorized agent to act on your behalf.
  • Not receive discriminatory treatment for exercising these rights.

California residents have specific rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA) — including the right to know, right to delete, right to correct, and right to opt out of "sharing" as defined in those laws. We do not sell personal data and we do not share it for cross-context behavioral advertising.

To exercise any of these rights, email privacy@13stars.media from the address on file, or write to us at the address in Section 1. We will respond within the timeframes required by applicable law.

9. Cookies and local storage

MyDash uses a small number of first-party cookies and browser local-storage entries required to keep you signed in, preserve your session, and remember UI preferences (e.g. theme, sidebar state). We do not use third-party advertising cookies in the MyDash staff application.

Consumer newsletter signup forms and public StellarPress pages may use a limited amount of analytics (e.g. anonymized page-view counts) as described in the respective site's own policy.

10. Security

We use industry-standard controls including encrypted connections (HTTPS), encrypted storage, role-based access control, least-privilege service tokens, audit logging, and routine access reviews. Administrative access to the underlying database is limited to a small number of authorized engineers and is logged.

No system is perfectly secure. If we become aware of a security incident that affects your personal data, we will notify you without undue delay as required by applicable law.

11. Children

MyDash is not directed to children under 13 (or under 16 where required by local law) and we do not knowingly collect personal data from them. If you believe we have inadvertently collected information from a child, contact us and we will delete it.

12. International users

MyDash's infrastructure is hosted in the United States. If you access the platform from outside the U.S., your personal data will be transferred to and processed in the United States under U.S. law.

13. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be announced on the MyDash sign-in page or by email to active users. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page shows when the policy was last revised.
